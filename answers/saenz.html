Bilingualism. People are either trying to learn Spanish or want to improve their Spanish language skills. It can be a complicated predicament, especially with racial tensions high and immigrant rights a hot topic of national discourse. Unfortunately, pressure to assimilate into the "American" culture often conflicts with the desire to embrace diversity and cultural competency, thereby moving bilingualism even further into the horizon for some.

Inclusiveness. Austin's future is multicultural, with the Hispanic culture being the most prominent. It's a numbers game, really, and the demographic forecast is quite clear. Austin has a long and complicated history with minority and underserved communities, and the impact of the Hispanic culture, along with all other cultures, will continue to push Austin to be more inclusive.
